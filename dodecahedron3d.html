Dodecahedron
A dodecahedron, from Greek for "twelve faces," is a polyhedron with twelve flat faces. The most familiar type is the regular dodecahedron, a Platonic solid with regular pentagonal faces and icosahedral symmetry. Variants include the pyritohedron and tetartoid, which share the same vertex and edge structure but have irregular faces. The rhombic dodecahedron, with octahedral symmetry, and its variations are space-filling. A unique feature of the regular dodecahedron is that one can draw infinite straight lines from a corner that return to the original point without crossing other corners.
